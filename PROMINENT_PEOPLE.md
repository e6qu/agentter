## Researchers and Academics

### Geoffrey Hinton
- **Role:** Professor Emeritus, University of Toronto; Former Google researcher
- **Known for:** "Godfather of Deep Learning", backpropagation, neural networks, AlexNet
- **Education:** Cambridge University (BA in Experimental Psychology), Edinburgh University (PhD in AI)
- **Recognition:** Turing Award (2018) with LeCun and Bengio; Nobel Prize in Physics (2024)

**Key Papers (200,000+ citations):**
- **"Learning Representations by Back-Propagating Errors"** (1986) - *Nature* 323, 533-536
  - Co-authored with Rumelhart and Williams
  - The foundational backpropagation paper (60,000+ citations)
- **"ImageNet Classification with Deep Convolutional Neural Networks"** (AlexNet, 2012) - NeurIPS
  - Co-authored with Alex Krizhevsky and Ilya Sutskever
  - 190,000+ citations; sparked deep learning revolution
- **"Deep Learning"** (2015) - *Nature* 521, 436-444
  - Co-authored with LeCun and Bengio
  - Comprehensive review (100,000+ citations)
- **"A Fast Learning Algorithm for Deep Belief Nets"** (2006) - Neural Computation 18, 1527-1554
  - Introduced layer-wise pre-training
- **"Reducing the Dimensionality of Data with Neural Networks"** (2006) - *Science* 313, 504-507
  - With Salakhutdinov; deep autoencoders
- **"Dropout: A Simple Way to Prevent Neural Networks from Overfitting"** (2014) - JMLR 15, 1929-1958
  - Co-authored with Srivastava, et al. (60,000+ citations)
- **"Distilling the Knowledge in a Neural Network"** (2015) - arXiv:1503.02531
  - Knowledge distillation ("dark knowledge")
- **"The Forward-Forward Algorithm"** (2022) - arXiv:2212.13345
  - Proposed alternative to backpropagation

**Notable Projects:**
- **DNNresearch Inc.** (2012): Co-founded with Krizhevsky and Sutskever; acquired by Google for $44M
- **Capsule Networks** (2017): Alternative to CNNs for spatial hierarchies
- **t-SNE** (2008): Visualization algorithm with Laurens van der Maaten
- **Boltzmann Machines**: Co-invented with Ackley and Sejnowski (1985)

**Books:**
- Various contributions to academic collections on neural networks and machine learning

**Famous quotes:**
> "I left so that I could talk about the dangers of AI without considering how this impacts Google."
> — New York Times interview, May 1, 2023; also tweet from @geoffreyhinton, May 1, 2023

> "I thought it was 30 to 50 years or even longer away. Obviously, I no longer think that."
> — New York Times interview, May 1, 2023 (on AGI timeline)

> "I've come to the conclusion that the kind of intelligence we're developing is very different from the intelligence we have... We're biological systems and these are digital systems."
> — BBC interview, May 2, 2023

> "I console myself with the normal excuse: If I hadn't done it, somebody else would have."
> — New York Times interview, May 1, 2023 (on regrets about AI work)

### Yann LeCun
- **Role:** Chief AI Scientist, Meta; Professor, NYU
- **Known for:** Convolutional neural networks (CNNs), "Godfather of AI"
- **Education:** ESPCI Paris (Engineer degree), Pierre and Marie Curie University (PhD in Computer Science)
- **Recognition:** Turing Award (2018) with Hinton and Bengio

**Key Papers (100,000+ citations):**
- **"Deep Learning"** (2015) - *Nature* 521, 436-444
  - Co-authored with Bengio and Hinton (100,000+ citations)
- **"Gradient-Based Learning Applied to Document Recognition"** (1998) - Proceedings of the IEEE 86(11), 2278-2324
  - LeNet-5 paper (80,000+ citations)
- **"Backpropagation Applied to Handwritten Zip Code Recognition"** (1989) - Neural Computation 1(4), 541-551
  - First practical application of backpropagation with CNNs (20,000+ citations)
- **"The MNIST Database of Handwritten Digits"** (1998)
  - Standard benchmark dataset (9,000+ citations)
- **"Deep Learning"** textbook chapter contributions
- **"A Tutorial on Energy-Based Learning"** (2006)
- **"Optimal Brain Damage"** (1990) - NeurIPS
  - With Denker and Solla; network pruning

**Notable Projects:**
- **LeNet** (1989-1998): Series of CNN architectures for handwritten digit recognition
  - LeNet-5 used by US Postal Service and banks for check reading
  - Processed 10-20% of all US checks (late 1990s)
- **DjVu** (1996): Document compression technology (co-developed at AT&T)
  - Used by Internet Archive for scanned documents
- **Lush**: Programming language for neural network development
- **Torch** (early 2000s): Deep learning framework (predecessor to PyTorch)
- **Meta AI Research**: Leading open-source AI development at Meta

**Presentations:**
- TED Talk: "How AI Could Save (Not Destroy) Humanity" (2024)
- Lex Fridman Podcast (March 2024)
- Regular keynotes at NeurIPS, ICML, CVPR

**Famous quotes:**
> "Once AI systems become more intelligent than humans, humans will *still* be the 'apex species'."
> — Reddit r/ControlProblem, quoted in community discussions; original source unclear

> "The Doomer's Delusion: 1. AI is likely to kill us all 2. Hence AI must be monopolized by a small number of companies under tight regulatory control."
> — Twitter/X post (@ylecun), May 2024

> "We are not going to get to human-level AI just by scaling LLMs... There's no way, absolutely no way, and whatever you can hear from some of my more adventurous colleagues, it's not going to happen within the next two years. There's absolutely no way in hell to–pardon my French."
> — Alex Kantrowitz's Big Technology podcast, Spring 2025

**Controversies:**
- Quit Twitter in June 2020 following acrimonious exchanges on AI bias
- Frequently clashes with AI safety advocates ("doomers")
- Accused by Gary Marcus of systematically failing to credit predecessors

### Yoshua Bengio
- **Role:** Professor, University of Montreal; Founder, Mila
- **Known for:** Deep learning foundations, generative adversarial thinking
- **Education:** McGill University (BEng, MEng, PhD in Computer Science)
- **Recognition:** Turing Award (2018) with Hinton and LeCun

**Key Papers:**
- **"Deep Learning"** (2015) - *Nature* 521, 436-444
  - Co-authored with LeCun and Hinton
- **"Generative Adversarial Nets"** (2014) - NeurIPS
  - Co-authored with Goodfellow, et al. (100,000+ citations)
- **"Neural Machine Translation by Jointly Learning to Align and Translate"** (2014) - arXiv:1409.0473
  - Attention mechanism for sequence models (40,000+ citations)
- **"Learning Long-Term Dependencies with Gradient Descent is Difficult"** (1994) - IEEE TNN 5(2), 157-166
  - Early work on gradient flow issues
- **"A Neural Probabilistic Language Model"** (2003) - JMLR 3, 1137-1155
  - Neural language models (12,000+ citations)
- **"Representation Learning: A Review and New Perspectives"** (2013) - IEEE TPAMI 35(8), 1798-1828
  - Co-authored with Courville and Vincent (19,000+ citations)
- **"Stochastic Parrots" paper** (2021) - co-authored "On the Dangers of Stochastic Parrots"
  - Influential critique of LLM scaling

**Books:**
- **"Deep Learning"** (2016) - MIT Press
  - Co-authored with Ian Goodfellow and Aaron Courville
  - The definitive deep learning textbook (free online at [deeplearningbook.org](http://www.deeplearningbook.org))

**Notable Projects:**
- **Mila** (Quebec AI Institute): Founded research institute
- **Theano** (2007-2017): Early deep learning framework
