### Demis Hassabis
- **Role:** CEO and Co-founder, Google DeepMind
- **Known for:** AlphaGo, AlphaFold, Gemini, chess prodigy, game developer
- **Background:** Chess prodigy (master standard at age 13, Elo 2300); neuroscientist; game developer (Theme Park at age 17, Black & White, Republic, Evil Genius); bought his first computer (ZX Spectrum 48K) with chess winnings and taught himself to program
- **Education:** Cambridge University (Computer Science), UCL (PhD in Cognitive Neuroscience)
- **Recognition:** Nobel Prize in Chemistry (2024) for AlphaFold protein structure predictions; TIME 100 (2017, 2025); TIME Person of the Year (2025, "Architects of AI"); Knighted (2024)

**Key Papers:**
- **"Highly Accurate Protein Structure Prediction with AlphaFold"** (2021) - *Nature* 596, 583-589
  - Co-authored with John Jumper, et al.
  - 44,000+ citations; solved 50-year protein folding problem
- **"Accurate Structure Prediction of Biomolecular Interactions with AlphaFold 3"** (2024) - *Nature* 630, 493-500
  - Extended AlphaFold to DNA/RNA and small molecules
- **"Human-level Control through Deep Reinforcement Learning"** (2015) - *Nature* 518, 529-533
  - DQN paper; breakthrough in Atari game playing
- **"Mastering the Game of Go with Deep Neural Networks and Tree Search"** (2016) - *Nature* 529, 484-489
  - AlphaGo paper; defeated Lee Sedol
- **"Mastering the Game of Go Without Human Knowledge"** (2017) - *Nature* 550, 354-359
  - AlphaGo Zero paper
- **"A General Reinforcement Learning Algorithm that Masters Chess, Shogi, and Go through Self-Play"** (2018) - *Science* 362, 1140-1144
  - AlphaZero paper
- **"Neuroscience-Inspired Artificial Intelligence"** (2017) - *Neuron* 95, 245-258
  - Co-authored with Kumaran, Summerfield, Botvinick

**Notable Projects:**
- **DeepMind** (co-founded 2010): AI research company acquired by Google (2014)
- **AlphaGo** (2016): First AI to defeat world champion at Go
- **AlphaFold** (2020): Solved protein structure prediction
  - [AlphaFold Protein Structure Database](https://alphafold.ebi.ac.uk/): 200M+ protein structures
  - [AlphaFold Server](https://alphafoldserver.com/): Free access for researchers
- **AlphaFold 3** (2024): Extended to all biomolecular interactions
- **Gemini** (2023-present): Google's multimodal AI model

**Presentations:**
- Nobel Prize Lecture (December 2024)
- Multiple TED Talks on AI and scientific discovery
- Royal Society lectures

**Notable quotes:**
> "My journey on AI started with games and specifically chess."
> — 2025, on how chess sparked his interest in intelligence

> "I would actually encourage kids to play games, but not just to play them... the most important thing is to try and make them."
> — 2025, on game development as a learning tool

**Why he matters:**
Hassabis represents the rarest combination in AI: a world-class researcher who is also a successful CEO. His path from chess prodigy to game developer to neuroscientist to DeepMind founder traces the intellectual lineage of modern AI. AlphaGo's victory over Lee Sedol in 2016 was the "Sputnik moment" for AI, and AlphaFold's solution to protein folding earned a Nobel Prize and demonstrated that AI can make transformative scientific discoveries. He now leads Google's entire AI effort through Gemini.

**Notable quotes:**

- 2025: Microsoft lost exclusive access to OpenAI's research under renegotiated deal; Nadella shifted to "founder mode," pushing internal AI development

## Industry Leaders

### Jensen Huang
- **Role:** Founder, President, and CEO, NVIDIA (since 1993)
- **Known for:** The leather jacket; CUDA bet; transforming NVIDIA from a gaming GPU company into the most important AI infrastructure company in the world; GTC keynotes
- **Background:** Born in Tainan, Taiwan (1963); moved to the US as a child; worked as a busboy and janitor; co-founded NVIDIA in 1993 at a Denny's restaurant at age 30
- **Education:** Oregon State University (BS in Electrical Engineering), Stanford University (MS in Electrical Engineering)
- **Net worth:** ~$125B+ (2026), making him one of the wealthiest people in the world

**Key strategic bets:**
- **CUDA** (2006): Turned GPUs into general-purpose computing tools; "When I launched CUDA, the audience was complete silence. Nobody wanted it. Nobody asked for it. Nobody understood it." This bet positioned NVIDIA for the AI era a decade before it arrived
- **Deep learning pivot** (2012): Recognized deep learning's potential after AlexNet; reoriented NVIDIA's roadmap around AI training
- **AI data center chips** (2016-present): A100, H100, Blackwell, Vera Rubin architectures
- **GTC 2026**: Announced $1 trillion+ revenue opportunity from 2025-2027; Vera Rubin platform described as "a generational leap" with 10x inference throughput per watt

**Notable quotes:**
> "When I launched CUDA, the audience was complete silence. Nobody wanted it. Nobody asked for it. Nobody understood it."
> — On the CUDA bet that made the AI revolution possible

> "You're not going to lose your job to an AI, but you're going to lose your job to somebody who uses AI."
> — 2025, Milken Conference

> "AI is infrastructure."
> — 2025, arguing every country should treat AI like electricity or roads

> "Finally, AI is able to do productive work, and therefore the inflection point of inference has arrived."
> — GTC 2026 keynote

**The leather jacket:**
Huang's signature black leather jacket has become iconic in tech — a symbol of NVIDIA's dominance. Mark Zuckerberg posted a photo of himself and Huang wearing each other's signature attire (Zuck's hoodie, Jensen's jacket) with the caption: "He's like Taylor Swift, but for tech" (March 2024).

**Why he matters:**
NVIDIA's GPUs power virtually every AI training run and an increasing share of inference. The company's market cap exceeded $3 trillion in 2024, briefly making it the most valuable company in the world. Huang's three-decade bet on accelerated computing — from gaming to scientific simulation to AI — is one of the most prescient strategic decisions in technology history. CUDA's ecosystem moat means that even well-funded competitors (AMD, Intel, custom chips) struggle to displace NVIDIA.

### Lisa Su
- **Role:** Chair and CEO, AMD (since 2014); Chair, Semiconductor Industry Association (elected November 2025)
- **Known for:** Turning AMD around from near-bankruptcy to a $200B+ company; MI300X AI accelerators challenging NVIDIA's dominance; engineering-first leadership
- **Background:** Born in Tainan, Taiwan (notably the same city as Jensen Huang, who is her distant relative — Huang is her mother's uncle's son); moved to the US at age 3
- **Education:** MIT (BS, MS, PhD in Electrical Engineering)

**Key strategic moves:**
- 2014: Became AMD CEO when the company was near bankruptcy (~$3B market cap)
- Executed turnaround through Zen CPU architecture and RDNA GPU architecture
- 2023: Launched MI300X AI accelerator with 192GB HBM3 memory (60% more bandwidth, 2x memory vs. NVIDIA H100)
- 2025: MI350 series with 35x inference improvement over MI300; targeting $5B+ in AI chip revenue
- 2026: MI400 series planned with 432GB HBM4 memory; Helios rack-scale solutions; CES 2026 keynote

**Notable quotes:**
> "Dream big, be bold, and take on the hardest problems."
> — Stanford GSB talk, personal motto

**Key partnerships:**
- Secured MI300X deployments from Microsoft, Meta, Oracle
- Targeting NVIDIA's 90%+ AI accelerator market share

**Why she matters:**
Su's turnaround of AMD is one of the greatest corporate comebacks in tech history. Under her leadership, AMD grew from ~$3B to $200B+ in market cap. In the AI era, AMD's MI300X is the most credible alternative to NVIDIA's dominance, and competition from AMD is essential to preventing a monopoly on AI compute. Her election as SIA Chair signals her influence across the entire semiconductor industry.

### Ian Goodfellow
- **Role:** Research Scientist, Google DeepMind
- **Known for:** Invented Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs); co-authored the "Deep Learning" textbook; "The GANfather"
- **Background:** PhD student of Yoshua Bengio at Universite de Montreal; invented GANs during a late night after a bar conversation (2014); worked at Google Brain, OpenAI (early employee), Apple (Director of ML), and Google DeepMind
- **Education:** Stanford University (BS and MS in Computer Science), Universite de Montreal (PhD in Machine Learning, advised by Yoshua Bengio and Aaron Courville)

**Key Papers:**

  - 100,000+ citations; introduced the GAN framework
  - Two neural networks (generator and discriminator) compete in a game-theoretic framework
- **"Explaining and Harnessing Adversarial Examples"** (2015) - ICLR
  - Introduced the Fast Gradient Sign Method (FGSM) for adversarial attacks
- **"Improved Techniques for Training GANs"** (2016) - NeurIPS

**Books:**
- **"Deep Learning"** (2016) - MIT Press
  - Co-authored with Yoshua Bengio and Aaron Courville
  - The definitive deep learning textbook; free online at [deeplearningbook.org](http://www.deeplearningbook.org)

**The GAN origin story:**
On May 26, 2014, at Les 3 Brasseurs (The Three Brewers) in Montreal, friends asked Goodfellow for help with a project generating photos. They were struggling with the computational challenge. Goodfellow realized it was "not a programming problem — it's an algorithm design problem." He conceived the idea of pitting a generator against a discriminator. Skeptical friends egged him on. He went home, coded it that night while his girlfriend slept, tested it — and it worked the first time. The story is often romanticized as "the idea on a bar napkin," though the actual genius was in the late-night implementation.

**Career moves:**
- OpenAI (2016-2017): One of the first employees
- Google Brain (2017-2019): Research scientist
- Apple (2019-2022): Director of ML in Special Projects Group; left reportedly over Apple's return-to-office mandate
- Google DeepMind (2023-present): Research scientist

**Notable quotes:**
> "AI fakes can be good enough that it's really hard to tell."
> — 2025, reflecting on how the theoretical possibilities of GANs have become reality

**Controversies:**
- Priority dispute with Jurgen Schmidhuber, who claims GANs are essentially his 1992 "Predictability Minimization" models run in reverse (see FAMOUS_SPATS.md)
- At NIPS 2016, Schmidhuber publicly confronted Goodfellow during his GAN tutorial

**Why he matters:**
GANs were one of the most important ideas in AI of the 2010s, enabling realistic image generation, style transfer, data augmentation, and laying groundwork for the generative AI revolution. While diffusion models have largely superseded GANs for image generation, the adversarial training framework remains influential. The "Deep Learning" textbook he co-authored trained an entire generation of ML practitioners.

### Georgi Gerganov
- **Role:** Creator of llama.cpp and GGML; joined Hugging Face (2026)
- **Known for:** Single-handedly enabling local LLM inference on consumer hardware; created the GGML tensor library and GGUF model format
- **Background:** Bulgarian software engineer; started GGML in September 2022; released llama.cpp in March 2023
- **Notable:** The "GG" in GGML and GGUF stands for his initials (Georgi Gerganov)

**Notable Projects:**
- **GGML** (September 2022): C library implementing tensor algebra with strict memory management and multi-threading
- **llama.cpp** (March 2023): Implementation of Llama inference in pure C/C++ with no dependencies
  - [GitHub: ggml-org/llama.cpp](https://github.com/ggml-org/llama.cpp)
  - Enabled running LLMs on consumer laptops, Raspberry Pis, and phones
  - Became the foundation of the local AI movement
- **GGUF** (August 2023): Model file format for distributing quantized LLMs
  - Became the de facto standard for local model distribution
  - Used by virtually every local AI application
- **Hugging Face acquisition** (February 2026): ggml.ai joined Hugging Face; Gerganov and team continue maintaining projects full-time; llama.cpp stays open-source and community-governed

**Why he matters:**
Gerganov democratized LLM access more than any single person. Before llama.cpp, running a large language model required expensive GPU clusters. His C/C++ implementation, combined with aggressive quantization techniques, meant that anyone with a laptop could run a capable language model locally. The GGUF format became the standard for model distribution in the local AI ecosystem. His work fundamentally challenged the assumption that AI must be centralized in the cloud.

### Eliezer Yudkowsky
- **Role:** Founder and Research Fellow, Machine Intelligence Research Institute (MIRI)
- **Known for:** AI alignment pioneer; rationality community; LessWrong founder; "doomer" figurehead; author of "Harry Potter and the Methods of Rationality" (HPMOR); most prominent advocate for the position that AI will likely cause human extinction

- **Education:** Self-educated; no university degree

**Key Writings:**

- **"If Anyone Builds It, Everyone Dies: Why Superhuman AI Would Kill Us All"** (2025) — Co-authored with Nate Soares; New York Times Best Seller
- **"Harry Potter and the Methods of Rationality"** (HPMOR, 2010-2015) — Harry Potter fanfiction that became a cult classic and recruiting tool for the rationality community; 660,000+ words
- **LessWrong** (founded 2009): Community blog focused on rationality, philosophy, and AI safety; spawned the "rationalist" community that produced many AI safety researchers

**Notable quotes:**
> "The AI does not hate you, nor does it love you, but you are made out of atoms which it can use for something else."
> — Widely cited formulation of the "instrumental convergence" concern

> "We do not have the technological capability to design a super intelligent AI that is polite, obedient and aligned with human intentions — and we are nowhere close to achieving that."
> — SXSW panel, "How to Make AGI Not Kill Everyone"

> "The first step is to lock down all the hardware supporting advanced AI development."
> — 2025, on proposed policy responses to AI risk

**Key beliefs:**
- AI alignment is unsolved and may be unsolvable before superintelligence arrives
- Current AI development is "racing toward a cliff"
- Advocates for international moratorium on frontier AI training runs

**Controversies:**
- Criticized for extreme pessimism that some argue paralyzes productive action
- "Doomer" label embraced by some supporters, rejected by critics as alarmist
- Ongoing disputes with Yann LeCun and other AI optimists (see FAMOUS_SPATS.md)
- TIME magazine interview (2023): Argued that bombing data centers might be justified to prevent extinction-level AI risk

**Why he matters:**
Whether you agree with him or not, Yudkowsky defined the intellectual framework for AI safety. The concepts of "alignment," "instrumental convergence," "mesa-optimization," and many other ideas central to AI safety discourse originated in or were popularized by his writing. LessWrong produced many of the researchers who now work at Anthropic, OpenAI, and DeepMind on safety. His extreme positions have the Overton window effect of making moderate AI safety concerns seem more reasonable.
