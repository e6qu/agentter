## The Moderators

### dang (Daniel Gackle)

**Profile:** https://news.ycombinator.com/user?id=dang  
**Joined:** 2011 (as gruseom), switched to dang in 2014  
**Role:** Lead moderator since 2014

The most visible moderator on HN, dang (Daniel Gackle) has become legendary for his patient, thoughtful moderation style. A Stanford literature major turned startup founder turned full-time moderator.

**Origin Story:**
Gackle co-founded a startup with Scott Bell (sctb) developing online spreadsheet software, funded by YC. When the startup failed, Paul Graham invited him to join YC full-time to manage HN.

**Notable quotes from moderation:**

> "The instinct that there simply has to be a better way to build systems, and the yearning to connect with it. If you can't do that at your job, and few can, then you can at least dream and read and argue about it on the Internet. Hacker News is the inverse image of many people's jobs, overlaid on top of each other—an escape valve for frustrated idealists."
> — dang, The New Yorker profile, 2019

> "There are still jokes, they just require higher levels of intelligence to parse now."
> — dang on HN humor

**Moderation style characteristics:**
- Personal, focused, and slow responses
- Merging duplicate submissions with context
- Linking to related past discussions
- Editing submission titles for accuracy
- Sending long explanatory emails to users
- Shadowbanning (secretly limiting visibility of problematic accounts)

**Community appreciation:**
- Annual "Thank You dang" threads (search: "thank you dang")
- Described as practicing "conversational art"
- Known for maintaining civility without heavy-handed bans
- Users report receiving thoughtful multi-paragraph emails explaining moderation decisions

### tomhow (Tom Howard)

Announced as a public moderator in early 2025. YC W09 batchmate, joined HN in 2007. Previously performed moderation duties behind the scenes for years.

### sctb (Scott Bell)

Former moderator who left around 2019. Co-founded a startup with dang that developed online spreadsheet software. Less visible than dang but instrumental in early moderation infrastructure.

## Famous Threads & Phenomena

### The "Who is Hiring?" Threads

**Pattern:** First of each month, posted by whoishiring bot

The most economically significant recurring thread on HN. A leading indicator of the tech job market.

**Structure:**
- Top-level comments: Job postings
- Format: Company | Location | Remote | Role | Salary
- Often 400+ comments in active months

**Notable analysis:**
- 2022 analysis showed lowest post count in 30 months during economic downturn
- Scrapers have analyzed 8+ years of data
- Startup hiring is often the first to freeze during downturns
- Consultant vs. freelancer supply/demand imbalances

**Community insight:**
> "If you're a contractor looking for work, I would target companies where software isn't their primary business."
> — HN user on freelancer thread dynamics

**The reverse thread:**
"Who wants to be hired?" - Engineers post their availability, often with impressive credentials.

### The "Eternal September" Resistance

HN has successfully resisted the "Eternal September" phenomenon that destroyed Usenet quality. Key factors:

1. **Strict ranking algorithm:** 4 upvotes needed to reach front page
2. **Karma thresholds:** 31 karma to flag, 251 to customize header color, 501 to downvote
3. **Human moderation:** dang's personal touch
4. **Cultural norms:** Self-enforced civility standards
5. **Text-based interface:** Discourages low-effort content

### The "HN Hug of Death"

When a small site reaches HN front page, the traffic surge can overwhelm servers. Legendarily:
- Can exhaust monthly bandwidth quotas in hours
- Has both helped and destroyed small projects
- Considered a badge of honor despite technical pain
- Often discussed as validation of interest

**Famous examples:**
- Side projects receiving 100k+ visits in hours
- AWS bills skyrocketing unexpectedly
- "Thank you for the hug of death" blog posts

### Legendary Individual Threads

| Thread | Year | Significance |
|--------|------|--------------|
| "Show HN: Dropbox" | 2007 | Drew Houston's original launch, YC application success story |
| "Show HN: GitHub" | 2008 | Pre-launch feedback, before it became dominant |
| "Show HN: Stripe" | 2009 | Payment processing for developers |
| "Falsehoods Programmers Believe About Names" | 2010 | patio11's most famous post, now canonical |
| "I analyzed 8 years of Who is Hiring data" | 2019 | Community data analysis |
| GPT/ChatGPT announcement threads | 2022-2023 | AI inflection point discussions |
| "Ask HN: Who is hiring?" monthly | Ongoing | Most important job board in tech |
| "Tell HN: I got fired" | Various | Support threads for fired employees |

### The "Deadpool" Phenomenon

HN tracks failed startups with particular interest:
- Post-mortem analyses
- "What went wrong" discussions
- Lessons learned threads
- Often more educational than success stories

## Community Rituals

### 1. "Show HN"

The community's product launch platform. Rules:
- Must be something you've made
- No press releases or third-party launches
- Expect critical but (usually) constructive feedback
- Best time to post: Tuesday-Thursday, 8-10am PT

**Famous "Show HN" successes:**
- Dropbox (Drew Houston)
- GitHub (Tom Preston-Werner)
- Stripe (Patrick & John Collison)
- Discord (Jason Citron)
- Notion
- Figma

**The lifecycle:**
1. Show HN post
2. Initial feedback (often critical)
3. Iteration based on feedback
4. Subsequent launches
5. HN tracks growth

### 2. "Ask HN"

Community advice threads covering:
- Career decisions
- Technical architecture
- Startup dilemmas
- Life advice
- "What happened to [company]?"

**Notable patterns:**
- "Ask HN: How do you X?" format
- Often attracts expert practitioners
- High signal-to-noise compared to other platforms
- Frequently indexed by search engines

**Famous Ask HN threads:**
- "Ask HN: What happened to [discontinued product]?"
- "Ask HN: How do you negotiate salary?"
- "Ask HN: What book changed your life?"
- "Ask HN: Side project to $X MRR?"

### 3. "Tell HN"

Informational posts, typically:
- Security disclosures
- Important project updates
- Community announcements
- Farewell posts ("I'm leaving [company]")

### 4. The "Second Chance Pool"

Moderators manually re-up interesting posts that didn't gain traction. Users receive emails like:

> "We thought you might like to know that we put [link] in the second-chance pool, so it will get a random placement on the front page sometime in the next 24 hours."

**Mechanism:**
- Mods can "re-up" posts by fabricating a timestamp that places them on the front page
- Prevents good content from being buried
- Source of occasional confusion ("Why does this say 3 days ago?")

### 5. Monthly Rituals

- **1st:** "Who is Hiring?" thread (by whoishiring bot)
- **1st:** "Who wants to be hired?" thread
- **1st:** "Freelancer? Seeking freelancer?" thread
- **Ad-hoc:** "Tell HN: I got laid off" support threads during downturns

### 6. The "Thank You dang" Threads

Annual ritual where users express appreciation for moderation:
- Usually around holidays
- Hundreds of comments
- dang typically responds humbly
- Ritualized community bonding

## Subcultures & In-Jokes

### The HN Tone

Described variously as:
- "Performative erudition" (The New Yorker)
- "A characteristically cerebral, detached quality"
- "Tech workers performing rationality"
- "Asperger's chic"

**Common tropes:**
- "This is obvious if you think about it"
- "Have you considered [obscure edge case]?"
- "Source?" (demanding citations)
- "HN is not [X]" (gatekeeping)
- "Citation needed"
- "I worked on this..."

### Recurring Debates (The "Holy Wars")

1. **Programming Languages:**
   - Rust evangelism vs. backlash ("Rust rewrite when?")
   - Go vs. Rust (simplicity vs. safety)
   - JavaScript ecosystem fatigue
   - "Why I'm leaving [language] for [language]"

2. **Editors:**
   - Vim vs. Emacs (classic, never dies)
   - VS Code adoption (Microsoft embrace/extend?)
   - Cursor/AI editors (emerging holy war)

3. **Work Culture:**
   - Remote vs. RTO (Return to Office)
   - FAANG compensation comparisons
   - Startup vs. Big Tech career paths
   - "Rest and vest" vs. "impact"

4. **Technology Choices:**
   - Nix (passionate defenders and detractors)
   - htmx vs. React (anti-JS framework movement)
   - Wayland vs. X11
   - Systemd debates

5. **Business Models:**
   - VC-funded vs. bootstrapped
   - Open source sustainability
   - Advertising ethics

### Meme Templates

**"HN in a nutshell":**
- "Interesting article about [topic]"
- Top comment: "Actually, the article misses [obscure technical detail]"
- Second comment: "I worked on this. Here's what really happened..."
- Third comment: "[Detailed criticism of implementation choices]"

**The HN Cycle:**
1. New technology announced
2. Enthusiastic adoption posts
3. "Why I switched back" posts
4. "HN is not representative" meta-commentary
5. Technology becomes mainstream
6. "Remember when we used [old tech]?" nostalgia

**"Tell HN" patterns:**
- "Tell HN: [Company] is shutting down"
- "Tell HN: I got fired"
- "Tell HN: [Security vulnerability]"

### The "Greybeard" Phenomenon

HN has a particular respect for users who:
- Have been in industry 20+ years
- Reference "AI winters" past
- Mention technologies younger users haven't heard of
- Provide historical context

### HN Bingo

Common phrases that appear in every thread:
- "This is why we can't have nice things"
- "Correlation is not causation"
- "HN is not your target market"
- "Move fast and break things"
- "Worse is better"
- "It depends"
- "Have you tried [obscure alternative]?"

## Controversies & Drama

### 1. The Thiel-Funded Lawsuit (Gawker)

HN discussion of Peter Thiel funding Hulk Hogan's lawsuit against Gawker deeply divided the community along free speech vs. privacy lines.

**Divisions:**
- Privacy advocates: Thiel was wronged, Gawker was malicious
- Free speech advocates: Dangerous precedent for wealthy silencing press

### 2. Google/Diversity Memo (2017)

James Damore's internal Google memo created one of the largest, most contentious threads in HN history.

**Moderation challenges:**
- Political flame wars
- Concerns about echo chambers
- Meta-discussions about HN's own biases
- Heavy dang intervention required

**Outcome:**
Thread locked, many sub-threads deleted, ongoing community division about whether HN handled it correctly.

### 3. Cryptocurrency Discussions

Bitcoin/crypto threads have historically been controversial:
- Early dismissal ("tulip bulbs", "greater fool theory")
- Later FOMO threads ("Am I too late?")
- Ongoing skepticism vs. advocacy
- Climate concerns vs. financial innovation

**Pattern:**
Every crypto bull run brings new HN users accusing the community of missing the boat.

### 4. The "YC Bias" Accusations

Regular accusations that HN favors YC companies:
- Mods manually boost YC posts (confirmed: second-chance pool applies to all)
- Cultural alignment with startup ethos
- Geographic concentration in SF Bay Area
- Selection bias in what gets submitted

**Counter-arguments:**
- YC companies are disproportionately interesting
- Selection bias at submission level, not moderation
- Non-YC companies regularly hit front page

### 5. Political Threads

The 2016 and 2020 US elections tested HN's apolitical norms:
- Heavy moderation required
- "Politics is mindkiller" (Yudkowsky reference)
- Attempts to contain culture war topics
- Accusations of bias from all sides

### 6. The "HN is Declining" Meta-Discussions

Regular threads (appearing every ~2 years):
- "Is HN getting worse?"
- "HN used to be better"
- "Too many [type of post] lately"

**Analysis:**
These threads themselves are a HN tradition dating back to at least 2010.

## The HN Aesthetic

### Visual Design Philosophy

HN's design is famously minimal:
- No images in comments
- Text-only interface
- Minimal CSS
- Functional over beautiful
- "HN Orange" (#ff6600) branding

**Why it works:**
- Reduces cognitive load
- Discourages low-effort content
- Fast loading
- Focus on substance
- Works on old browsers

**The anti-design:**
HN's ugliness is a feature. It:
- Repels users seeking entertainment
- Attracts users seeking information
- Forces focus on text
- Signals seriousness

### The Arc Language

HN runs on Arc, a Lisp dialect created by Paul Graham and Robert Morris:
- Open source but not widely adopted
- Minimalist philosophy
- Powers HN's unique features
- Source available at arclanguage.org

### Karma Culture

Unlike Reddit karma, HN karma:
- Is hidden on individual comments
- Serves as reputation, not currency
- Unlocks features (flagging, downvoting)
- Is not displayed prominently
- Cannot be traded or gamified

## Advanced Reading Techniques

### Tools & Techniques

**hnrss.org:**
- Filtered RSS feeds by topic/score
- "Best Comments" feed for high-quality discussion
- User-specific feeds

**Algolia Search:**
- `https://hn.algolia.com/`
- Search by domain, user, or topic
- Historical thread discovery
- Date range filtering

**API Access:**
- Official API: `https://github.com/HackerNews/API`
- Algolia API for search
- BigQuery public dataset (full history)

**Browser Extensions:**
- Newsit: Shows HN discussions for current page
- HN Enhancement Suite: Comment collapsing, user highlighting
- Various userscripts for customization

### Comment Reading Strategy

From community member hsu.cy:

> "For the first top-level comment, I read at most the first three replies and their first three sub-replies. Then I move to the second top-level comment and read its first two replies and their first two sub-replies, and thereafter... Finally, I scroll to the bottom and peek at the controversial or newer comments."

**The "Depth vs. Breadth" approach:**
1. Read top 3 top-level comments fully
2. Skim next 5 for variety
3. Check "More" link for hidden gems
4. Read collapsed comments if topic is contentious
5. Check user profiles of insightful commenters

### Identifying Quality

**Green Flags:**
- Technical depth with citations
- "I worked on this" firsthand accounts
- Nuanced consideration of tradeoffs
- Historical context
- Specific numbers and dates
- Acknowledgment of limitations

**Red Flags:**
- Confident assertions without credentials
- Political talking points
- Dismissive tone without engagement
- "HN is not representative" (while being on HN)
- "This will never work" (without explanation)
- Excessive use of jargon

**The patio11 Test:**
Would patio11 find this comment helpful? If not, reconsider posting.

### HN Archaeology

**Finding historical context:**
- Use Algolia search with date ranges
- Look for "Previously: [link]" from dang
- Check user's comment history
- Search for dead companies/products
- Read "What happened to..." threads

**Understanding company histories:**
- Search company name + "Show HN"
- Look for founder usernames
- Check "Who is Hiring" participation
- Read post-mortem threads
