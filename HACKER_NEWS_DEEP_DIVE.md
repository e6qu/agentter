## The Moderators

### dang (Daniel Gackle)

**Profile:** https://news.ycombinator.com/user?id=dang

The most visible moderator on HN, dang (Daniel Gackle) has become legendary for his patient, thoughtful moderation style. A Stanford literature major turned startup founder turned full-time moderator.

**Notable quotes from moderation:**

> "The instinct that there simply has to be a better way to build systems, and the yearning to connect with it. If you can't do that at your job, and few can, then you can at least dream and read and argue about it on the Internet. Hacker News is the inverse image of many people's jobs, overlaid on top of each other—an escape valve for frustrated idealists."
> — dang, The New Yorker profile, 2019

**Moderation style characteristics:**
- Personal, focused, and slow responses
- Merging duplicate submissions with context
- Linking to related past discussions
- Editing submission titles for accuracy
- Sending long explanatory emails to users

**Community appreciation:**
- Annual "Thank You dang" threads (search: "thank you dang")
- Described as practicing "conversational art"
- Known for maintaining civility without heavy-handed bans

### tomhow (Tom Howard)

Announced as a public moderator in early 2025. YC W09 batchmate, joined HN in 2007. Previously performed moderation duties behind the scenes.

### sctb (Scott Bell)

Former moderator who left around 2019. Co-founded a startup with dang that developed online spreadsheet software.

## Famous Threads & Phenomena

### The "Who is Hiring?" Threads

**Pattern:** First of each month, posted by whoishiring bot

The most economically significant recurring thread on HN. A leading indicator of the tech job market.

**Notable analysis:**
- 2022 analysis showed lowest post count in 30 months during economic downturn
- Scrapers have analyzed 8+ years of data
- Startup hiring is often the first to freeze during downturns

**Community insight:**
> "If you're a contractor looking for work, I would target companies where software isn't their primary business."
> — HN user on freelancer thread dynamics

### The "Eternal September" Resistance

HN has successfully resisted the "Eternal September" phenomenon that destroyed Usenet quality. Key factors:

1. **Strict ranking algorithm:** 4 upvotes needed to reach front page
2. **Karma thresholds:** 31 karma to flag, 501 to downvote
3. **Human moderation:** dang's personal touch
4. **Cultural norms:** Self-enforced civility standards

### The "HN Hug of Death"

When a small site reaches HN front page, the traffic surge can overwhelm servers. Legendarily:
- Can exhaust monthly bandwidth quotas in hours
- Has both helped and destroyed small projects
- Considered a badge of honor despite technical pain

## Community Rituals

### 1. "Show HN"

The community's product launch platform. Rules:
- Must be something you've made
- No press releases or third-party launches
- Expect critical but (usually) constructive feedback

**Famous "Show HN" successes:**
- Dropbox
- GitHub
- Stripe
- Discord

### 2. "Ask HN"

Community advice threads covering:
- Career decisions
- Technical architecture
- Startup dilemmas
- Life advice

**Notable patterns:**
- "Ask HN: How do you X?" format
- Often attracts expert practitioners
- High signal-to-noise compared to other platforms

### 3. "Tell HN"

Informational posts, typically:
- Security disclosures
- Important project updates
- Community announcements

### 4. The "Second Chance Pool"

Moderators manually re-up interesting posts that didn't gain traction. Users receive emails like:

> "We thought you might like to know that we put [link] in the second-chance pool, so it will get a random placement on the front page sometime in the next 24 hours."

### 5. Monthly Rituals

- **1st:** "Who is Hiring?" thread
- **1st:** "Who wants to be hired?" thread
- **1st:** "Freelancer? Seeking freelancer?" thread

## Subcultures & In-Jokes

### The HN Tone

Described variously as:
- "Performative erudition" (The New Yorker)
- "A characteristically cerebral, detached quality"
- "Tech workers performing rationality"

**Common tropes:**
- "This is obvious if you think about it"
- "Have you considered [edge case]?"
- "Source?" (demanding citations)
- "HN is not [X]" (gatekeeping)

### Recurring Debates (The "Holy Wars")

1. **Programming Languages:**
   - Rust evangelism vs. backlash
   - Go vs. Rust
   - JavaScript ecosystem fatigue

2. **Editors:**
   - Vim vs. Emacs (classic)
   - VS Code adoption
   - Cursor/AI editors (emerging)

3. **Work Culture:**
   - Remote vs. RTO (Return to Office)
   - FAANG compensation
   - Startup vs. Big Tech

4. **Technology Choices:**
   - Nix (passionate defenders and detractors)
   - htmx vs. React
   - Wayland vs. X11

### Meme Templates

**"HN in a nutshell":**
- "Interesting article about [topic]"
- Top comment: "Actually, the article misses [obscure technical detail]"
- Second comment: "I worked on this. Here's what really happened..."
- Third comment: "[Detailed criticism of implementation]"

**The HN Cycle:**
1. New technology announced
2. Enthusiastic adoption posts
3. "Why I switched back" posts
4. "HN is not representative" meta-commentary

## Controversies & Drama

### 1. The Thiel-Funded Lawsuit (Gawker)

HN discussion of Peter Thiel funding Hulk Hogan's lawsuit against Gawker deeply divided the community along free speech vs. privacy lines.

### 2. Google/Diversity Memo (2017)

James Damore's internal Google memo created one of the largest, most contentious threads in HN history. Moderation challenges included:
- Political flame wars
- Concerns about echo chambers
- Meta-discussions about HN's own biases

### 3. Cryptocurrency Discussions

Bitcoin/crypto threads have historically been controversial:
- Early dismissal ("tulip bulbs")
- Later FOMO threads
- Ongoing skepticism vs. advocacy

### 4. The "YC Bias" Accusations

Regular accusations that HN favors YC companies:
- Mods manually boost YC posts (confirmed: second-chance pool applies to all)
- Cultural alignment with startup ethos
- Geographic concentration in SF Bay Area

### 5. Political Threads

The 2016 and 2020 US elections tested HN's apolitical norms:
- Heavy moderation required
- "Politics is mindkiller" (Yudkowsky reference)
- Attempts to contain culture war topics

## Reading HN Effectively

### Tools & Techniques

**hnrss.org:**
- Filtered RSS feeds by topic/score
- "Best Comments" feed for high-quality discussion

**Algolia Search:**
- `https://hn.algolia.com/`
- Search by domain, user, or topic
- Historical thread discovery

**API Access:**
- Official API: `https://github.com/HackerNews/API`
- Algolia API for search
- BigQuery public dataset

**Browser Extensions:**
- Newsit: Shows HN discussions for current page
- Various HN enhancers (comment collapsing, etc.)

### Comment Reading Strategy

From community member hsu.cy:

> "For the first top-level comment, I read at most the first three replies and their first three sub-replies. Then I move to the second top-level comment and read its first two replies and their first two sub-replies, and thereafter... Finally, I scroll to the bottom and peek at the controversial or newer comments."

### Quality Indicators

**Green Flags:**
- Technical depth with citations
- "I worked on this" firsthand accounts
- Nuanced consideration of tradeoffs
- Historical context

**Red Flags:**
- Confident assertions without credentials
- Political talking points
- Dismissive tone without engagement
- "HN is not representative" (while being on HN)
