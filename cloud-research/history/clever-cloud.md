# Clever Cloud — A Sourced History

A chronological, fully-sourced history of **Clever Cloud**, the **Nantes, France**
Platform-as-a-Service (PaaS) founded in 2010 around **automatic scaling** and
**pay-as-you-go** application hosting, which over the 2020s repositioned itself as a
**European, digital-sovereignty-focused** cloud provider — and a notable example of a
**largely bootstrapped** infrastructure company.

Every non-trivial claim below carries a working source link. Primary sources (Clever
Cloud's own blog/press posts, founders' statements, the European Commission) are
preferred, with reputable outlets (Rude Baguette, FrenchWeb, Data Center Dynamics,
Help Net Security) as corroboration. Much of the coverage is French-language; French
primary sources are quoted and translated carefully. Where a detail could not be
confirmed against a primary or reputable secondary source, it is explicitly flagged
with **Could not verify:**. Controversies and characterizations are attributed to
their sources, with fact separated from reported claim.

> **Note on the brief's "2021/2022 funding raise":** No major venture round in
> 2021–2022 was located. Clever Cloud is consistently described as **bootstrapped**
> (its only documented external equity is an early **Kima Ventures** seed, ~2010–2012;
> amount undisclosed/"confidential"). The 2021–2022 period instead saw **executive
> hires** (a CTO and COO) and **sovereignty partnerships**, not a headline raise. See
> **Funding** and the "Could not verify" note.

## Timeline

### Founding (2010, Nantes)

- **2010 — Clever Cloud founded in Nantes by a team of video-game-minded developers.**
  Clever Cloud was created in **2010 in Nantes** by **Quentin Adam** (CEO) together
  with co-founders. Per a French startup-history profile, the founders — **Quentin,
  Julien, Marc-Antoine and Clément Nivolle** — were "développeurs passionnés de jeux
  vidéo" ("developers passionate about video games") who originally set out to build a
  game, found the path didn't go as planned, and discovered a real market in
  **automating software deployment**.
  - Sources: [Découvrez l'histoire de la startup Clever Cloud — J'aime les startups](https://www.jaimelesstartups.fr/clever-cloud/);
    [About Clever Cloud — Clever Cloud](https://www.clever.cloud/about/);
    [France's Cloud Startups (series): Introducing Clever Cloud — Rude Baguette (2012)](https://www.rudebaguette.com/en/2012/06/frances-cloud-startups-series-introducing-clever-cloud/).
  - The founding year (**2010**) and **Nantes** base are repeated in Clever Cloud's own
    later posts (e.g. "Founded in 2010, Clever Cloud is a company based in Nantes
    specialising in IT automation").
    - Source: [Clever Cloud appoints Steven Le Roux as CTO and Cédric Biron as COO — Clever Cloud (Jan 2022)](https://www.clever.cloud/blog/company/2022/01/04/clever-cloud-appoints-steven-le-roux-as-cto-and-cedric-biron-as-coo-to-continue-its-structuring/).

  - **Clément Nivolle** — co-founder (his LinkedIn headline reads "Co founder of Clever

    - Source (search-indexed LinkedIn headline): [Clément Nivolle — LinkedIn](https://fr.linkedin.com/in/cnivolle).

  - **Julien Durillon** and **Marc-Antoine Perennou** — named among the founding team in

    - Sources: [J'aime les startups (above)](https://www.jaimelesstartups.fr/clever-cloud/);

### Public launch of the PaaS (2012)

- **June 2012 (beta) → broader release.** As of a June 2012 interview, Clever Cloud was
  "in beta phase with a smaller scale release," planning "a more broad release later
  this summer," pitched against variable consumption, environmental impact, financial
  inefficiency, and infrastructure complexity.
  - Source: [Rude Baguette (Jun 2012)](https://www.rudebaguette.com/en/2012/06/frances-cloud-startups-series-introducing-clever-cloud/).

- **October 17, 2012 — Clever Cloud announces its PaaS with automatic scalability and a
  Kima Ventures fundraising.** After "two years of R&D," the Nantes startup released its
  PaaS, introducing — by its own framing — a **pay-as-you-go** payment model and
  **automatic scalability** of hosting performance, with support for **PHP, Java, Ruby
  and Scala** "without any code modification." CEO **Quentin Adam**: *"Les développeurs
  de nos clients peuvent aujourd'hui s'affranchir…"* ("Our customers' developers can now
  get rid of hosting management, and focus on their core business").
  - Source: [Clever Cloud releases PaaS cloud service with automatic scalability and announces a fundraising from Kima Ventures — Clever Cloud (Oct 17, 2012)](https://clever.cloud/blog/press/2012/10/17/clever-cloud-launch/)
    *(originally published at `clever-cloud.com/blog/press/2012/10/17/clever-cloud-launch/`,
    which 301-redirects to the `clever.cloud` host).*

- **The Kima Ventures seed.** The early backer was **Kima Ventures** (the personal
  fund of **Xavier Niel** and **Jérémie Berrebi**). The investment was reported as made
  "au début de l'année 2012" ("at the beginning of 2012"); the **amount was
  confidential** ("confidentiel"), with Berrebi noting it exceeded Kima's standard
  ticket sizes.
  - Source: [Le Nantais Clever Cloud lève des fonds auprès de Kima Ventures — FrenchWeb (Oct 17, 2012)](https://www.frenchweb.fr/le-nantais-clever-cloud-leve-des-fonds-aupres-de-kima-ventures/81881).
  - **Could not verify:** the **exact amount** of the Kima Ventures round (reported as
    confidential) and a clean separation of "2010 seed" vs "early-2012 round" — secondary
    aggregators list multiple Kima-related entries (≈2010–2012) but without confirmed
    dated primary amounts. See "Could not verify."

### The bootstrapped trajectory (2010s–2020s)

- **Largely bootstrapped growth.** Beyond the early Kima Ventures seed, Clever Cloud is
  repeatedly characterized as having grown **without venture capital**, reaching ~**$6.2M
  revenue** and **1,000+ customers** by 2024 on a bootstrapped basis, with headcount in
  the dozens (reported ~73 employees; Clever Cloud's own About page cites "90+ People"
  across Paris, Nantes, Lille, Lyon, Brest and remote).
  - Sources: [Clever Cloud Revenue 2024: $6.2M ARR (Bootstrapped) — GetLatka](https://getlatka.com/companies/clever-cloud);
    [About Clever Cloud — Clever Cloud](https://www.clever.cloud/about/).
  - **Caveat:** The $6.2M revenue / 1,000-customer / headcount figures originate from
    **GetLatka** (a third-party revenue-estimate database, partly self-reported), not
    audited filings — treat as **reported estimates**, not company-audited facts.

### Executive structuring (2021–2022)

- **March 2021 → January 2022 — new CTO and COO.** **Cédric Biron** joined in **March
  2021** as a project manager and was promoted to **COO** (announced January 2022);
  **Steven Le Roux** joined on **January 3, 2022** as **CTO**, bringing infrastructure /
  PaaS experience from **Orange** and **OVHcloud**. Clever Cloud framed these as
  "structuring" hires to support growth.
  - Source: [Clever Cloud appoints Steven Le Roux as CTO and Cédric Biron as COO — Clever Cloud (Jan 2022)](https://www.clever.cloud/blog/company/2022/01/04/clever-cloud-appoints-steven-le-roux-as-cto-and-cedric-biron-as-coo-to-continue-its-structuring/).

### Sovereignty positioning and the Scaleway partnership (2022–2023)

- **The "France/Europe cloud sovereignty" narrative.** Through the early 2020s, Clever
  Cloud (and Adam personally, via the Open Internet Project) became a vocal proponent of
  **European digital sovereignty** — emphasizing European jurisdiction, ownership and
  the ability to operate independently of non-EU providers.
  - Sources: [Bio — Quentin Adam — Les Annales des Mines](https://annales-des-mines.org/bio-adam-quentin/);
    [Sovereign Cloud — Clever Cloud](https://www.clever.cloud/sovereign-cloud/).

- **October 17, 2022 — sovereign data-center hardware alliance.** Clever Cloud joined
  **2CRSi, Kalray and Vates** in an initiative for "Sovereign and Innovative Data Center
  Equipment."
  - Source: [2CRSi, Clever Cloud, Kalray and Vates join forces for Sovereign and Innovative Data Center Equipment — Clever Cloud (Oct 17, 2022)](https://www.clever.cloud/blog/press/2022/10/17/2crsi-clever-cloud-kalray-and-vates-join-forces-for-sovereign-and-innovative-data-center-equipment/).

- **January 17, 2023 — Clever Cloud × Scaleway "sovereign European PaaS."** Clever Cloud
  and **Scaleway** (Iliad group) launched **"Web Platform – powered by Clever Cloud,"**
  a PaaS hosted in Scaleway's **DC5** data center (marketed as carbon-neutral, "30–40%
  less energy and 90% less water" than conventional DCs), positioned as a **sovereign**
  alternative for deploying web apps. CEO **Quentin Adam**: *"Web Platform allows users
  to benefit from the compatibility of the two providers' catalogs, and the increased
  possibilities of this bold, innovative and sovereign offering."* Scaleway's **Gaspard
  Plantrou** (SVP PaaS) called it "another milestone toward the simplified adoption of
  the cloud in a sovereign environment."
  - Sources: [Clever Cloud and Scaleway join forces to unveil a sovereign, European, PaaS offering — Clever Cloud (Jan 17, 2023)](https://www.clever.cloud/blog/press/2023/01/17/clever-cloud-and-scaleway-join-forces-to-unveil-a-sovereign-european-paas-offering/);
    [Scaleway and Clever Cloud join forces… — Scaleway](https://www.scaleway.com/en/news/scaleway-and-clever-cloud-join-forces-to-unveil-a-sovereign-european-paas-offering/).

### Own database products: the Materia family (2024–2025)

- **April 2024 (Devoxx France) — Materia unveiled.** Clever Cloud previewed **MateriaDB
  KV** (and Materia Functions) at **Devoxx Paris/France**, a serverless database family
  built on the **FoundationDB** open-source transactional engine (the same engine used by
  Apple iCloud and Snowflake).
  - Source: [MateriaDB KV, Functions: discover the future of Clever Cloud at Devoxx Paris 2024 — Clever Cloud (Apr 16, 2024)](https://www.clever.cloud/blog/company/2024/04/16/materiadb-kv-materia-functions/).

- **June 11, 2024 — Materia KV available to all.** Clever Cloud released **Materia KV**,
  "a key-value database synchronously replicated across our three Paris datacenters,"
  built on **FoundationDB**, with the guarantee that "When our servers respond, your data
  has been written three times."
  - Source: [Materia KV: our easy-to-use serverless key-value database is available to all — Clever Cloud (Jun 11, 2024)](https://www.clever.cloud/blog/features/2024/06/11/materia-kv-our-easy-to-use-serverless-key-value-database-is-available-to-all/).

- **February 6–7, 2025 — Clever Cloud Fest (15th anniversary), 11 new products.** For its
  **15th anniversary**, Clever Cloud held the **Clever Cloud Fest** in Nantes and
  announced **11 new products**, including **Materia KV JSON** (Redis-compatible JSON
  store), **Materia TS** (a time-series engine based on **Warp10**), **Clever AI**
  (multi-vendor/multi-model AI platform), **Clever Functions** (WebAssembly-based FaaS),
  **CKE** (a Kubernetes solution), **Clever KMS**, Keycloak-as-a-Service, Wireguard-based
  **Network Groups**, and a Xen-based Hypervisor-as-a-Service. Adam, on Clever AI: "we
  offer our customers a **sovereign, out-of-the-box** solution for integrating AI into
  their business processes."
  - Source: [Clever Cloud announces 11 new products at its Clever Cloud Fest — Clever Cloud (Feb 18, 2025)](https://clever.cloud/blog/press/2025/02/18/clever-cloud-announces-new-products-at-clever-cloud-fest/).

### EU sovereign-cloud procurement award (April 2026)

- **April 17, 2026 — EU Commission Sovereign Cloud award; Clever Cloud among winners.**
  The **European Commission** awarded its **Sovereign Cloud** call for tender — up to
  **€180 million over six years** for EU institutions to procure sovereign cloud
  services — to **four providers**: **Post Telecom** (Luxembourg) leading a partnership
  with **OVHcloud** and **Clever Cloud**; **STACKIT** (Schwarz Group); **Scaleway**
  (Iliad); and **Proximus** with **S3NS, Clarence and Mistral**. The Post Telecom /
  OVHcloud / Clever Cloud bid, STACKIT and Scaleway each reached **SEAL-3 ("Digital
  Resilience")** under the Commission's new Cloud Sovereignty Framework; Proximus reached
  **SEAL-2**.
  - Sources: [Commission advances cloud sovereignty through strategic procurement — European Commission (Apr 17, 2026)](https://commission.europa.eu/news-and-media/news/commission-advances-cloud-sovereignty-through-strategic-procurement-2026-04-17_en);
    [EU Commission selects four cloud providers under €180m sovereign cloud tender — Data Center Dynamics](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/eu-commission-selects-four-cloud-providers-under-180m-sovereign-cloud-tender/);
    [EU pushes for stronger cloud sovereignty, awards €180 million to four providers — Help Net Security (Apr 20, 2026)](https://www.helpnetsecurity.com/2026/04/20/eu-sovereign-cloud-tender-180-million-eu/).

## Controversies & characterizations

> Clever Cloud has a comparatively low-controversy public profile (no major breach,
> free-tier-removal, or shutdown saga). The notable themes are **positioning debates**,
> attributed to their sources.

### 1. "Sovereignty" framing as positioning

- **Fact:** Clever Cloud explicitly markets a **sovereign, European** PaaS and won an
  EU sovereign-cloud tender slot (SEAL-3) in April 2026.
  [European Commission (Apr 17, 2026)](https://commission.europa.eu/news-and-media/news/commission-advances-cloud-sovereignty-through-strategic-procurement-2026-04-17_en)
- **Characterization (Adam's own advocacy):** Adam argues that some "sovereign" cloud
  constructs reinforce non-EU monopolies — e.g. the headline of an InformatiqueNews
  interview quotes him: *"Quand on crée un Bleu ou un S3NS, on renforce d'abord un
  monopole"* ("When you create a [Bleu] or an S3NS, you first reinforce a monopoly"),
  a pointed take on France's US-hyperscaler-backed "sovereign" joint ventures. This is
  **his stated opinion**, not a neutral finding.
  [InformatiqueNews](https://www.informatiquenews.fr/quand-on-cree-un-bleu-ou-un-s3ns-on-renforce-dabord-un-monopole-quentin-adam-clever-cloud-105343)

### 2. Bootstrapped status vs. third-party financials

- **Fact / reported:** Clever Cloud is widely described as **bootstrapped** with
  ~$6.2M revenue and 1,000+ customers (2024). These specific figures come from
  **GetLatka**, a third-party (partly self-reported) database — not audited filings —
  so the precise numbers are **reported estimates**, not company-audited facts.
  [GetLatka](https://getlatka.com/companies/clever-cloud)
