# Aptible: A Sourced History

A chronological, fully-sourced history of **Aptible** — the New York–based,
security- and compliance-first **Platform as a Service (PaaS)** that began life as
the "**Heroku for regulated/healthcare workloads**": a Docker-based container
platform (originally **Enclave**) that bakes in the controls needed for **HIPAA**,
**SOC 2**, **ISO 27001**, and similar regimes. The company later added a
security-compliance management product (**Gridiron**, rebranded **Comply**),
ultimately exited that GRC business, refocused on its **Deploy** PaaS, and was
acquired by **Opti9 Technologies** in November 2025.

Every non-trivial claim below carries a working source link. Primary sources
(Aptible's own blog/press, Y Combinator, the acquirer's release) are preferred,
with reputable outlets (TechCrunch, GlobeNewswire/Yahoo Finance, Xconomy, etc.) as
corroboration. Items that could not be confirmed are marked **"Could not verify."**

> **Note on the Y Combinator batch.** The research brief asked to verify a

> Y Combinator's own blog post title (**"Aptible (YC S14)"**) state the batch was

### 2014 — Y Combinator (S14), Rock Health, and the seed round

- **Summer 2014 — Y Combinator (S14) and Rock Health.** Aptible went through
  **Y Combinator's Summer 2014 batch** and was simultaneously in a **Rock Health**
  (digital-health accelerator) class. TechCrunch (Aug 5, 2014) reported Aptible
  "is in the current classes of both the Rock Health and Y Combinator startup
  accelerators," and Y Combinator's own blog post is titled **"Aptible (YC S14)."**
  Sources: [TechCrunch (Aug 5, 2014)](https://techcrunch.com/2014/08/05/backed-by-yc-and-rock-health-aptible-handles-the-hard-parts-of-hipaa-compliance/);
  [Aptible (YC S14) — Y Combinator blog](https://www.ycombinator.com/blog/aptible-yc-s14-handles-the-hard-parts-of-hipaa-compliance/);
  [Aptible — Y Combinator](https://www.ycombinator.com/companies/aptible).

- **The original pitch: "the hard parts of HIPAA."** At launch Aptible combined a
  plain-English, "TurboTax-style" compliance questionnaire with Heroku-like
  infrastructure management, letting healthcare/SaaS developers reach HIPAA
  compliance "in an afternoon" rather than the traditional 200+ hours and
  $50,000–$100,000 of consulting. TechCrunch reported the company was running
  customers since late March 2014, had three full-time staff, and listed pricing
  at **$3,499/month** on an annual contract.
  Source: [TechCrunch (Aug 5, 2014)](https://techcrunch.com/2014/08/05/backed-by-yc-and-rock-health-aptible-handles-the-hard-parts-of-hipaa-compliance/).

- **August 18, 2014 — Seed round.** Aptible raised a **$120,000 seed round** dated
  **August 18, 2014**, associated with the Rock Health / Y Combinator cohorts.
  Sources: [Seed Round — Aptible (2014-08-18) — Crunchbase](https://www.crunchbase.com/funding_round/aptible-seed--7ae9dc27);
  [From Autism to Diabetes to HIPAA, Rock Health Seeds 8 More Startups — Xconomy (Aug 18, 2014)](https://web.archive.org/web/20210414050051/https://xconomy.com/san-francisco/2014/08/18/from-autism-to-diabetes-to-hipaa-rock-health-seeds-8-more-startups/).
  *(The **$120,000** figure and **Aug 18, 2014** date are from Crunchbase; the
  Rock Health portfolio addition the same day is corroborated by Xconomy.
  Additional named angels — Peter Livingston, Palm Drive Capital, Fresh VC —
  appear in secondary profiles but **could not be confirmed** against a primary
  source. **Could not verify** the full investor list.)*

### 2014–2016 — Enclave: the compliant container PaaS

- **Enclave — a HIPAA/SOC 2-compliant container PaaS.** Aptible's core product,
  **Enclave**, is a Docker-based deployment platform that provisions an AWS **VPC**
  on the customer's behalf and runs their Docker containers and databases inside
  it, with security controls (audit logging, encryption at rest/in transit,
  intrusion detection, isolated per-app environments, vulnerability scans) built in
  to satisfy **HIPAA, SOC 2, ISO 27001, GDPR**, and related frameworks. Aptible
  describes Enclave as "a mature, twelve-factor app deployment platform, with
  security and compliance features built-in" — i.e., a security/compliance-oriented
  analog to Heroku for regulated workloads.
  Sources: [Aptible Enclave — Container Orchestration Platform with Built-in Security Controls (archived Aptible page)](https://d2w6hjbchjr8wd.cloudfront.net/enclave/);
  [Aptible — homepage](https://www.aptible.com/).

### 2017 — Gridiron: security & compliance management

- **April 2017 — Gridiron launches (beta).** Aptible introduced **Gridiron**, a
  **security and compliance management** platform that helps teams design, operate,
  and audit an information-security management system (ISMS). Aptible's January
  2017 webinar introduced "Gridiron Security and Compliance Management Platform"
  alongside Enclave updates; by **April 2017** Aptible was helping AWS-deployed
  customers manage security/compliance via Gridiron, with standalone pricing cited
  at **$1,999/month** (annual) plus a discounted beta.
  Sources: [Introducing Gridiron Security and Compliance Management Platform … (Aptible Jan 2017 webinar) — SlideShare](https://www.slideshare.net/aptible/introducing-gridiron-security-and-compliance-management-platform-and-enclave-app-deployment-platform-updates-aptible-january-2017-webinar);
  [Recap: Aptible April 2017 Quarterly Product Update — Aptible](https://web.archive.org/web/2id_/https://www.aptible.com/blog/recap-aptible-april-2017-quarterly-product-update).

### ~2018–2019 — Rebrand: Enclave → Deploy, Gridiron → Comply

- **Brand overhaul.** Aptible renamed **Enclave** to **Aptible Deploy** and
  **Gridiron** to **Aptible Comply** as part of a brand-identity overhaul,
  explaining that after roughly five years and hundreds of companies the original
  "sports/team/strategy/security metaphor" names "often led to confusion."
  Sources: [Introducing Aptible's new brand identity — Aptible](https://app-28098.on-aptible.com/blog/introducing-new-brand-identity/);
  [Aptible Brand Strategy & Redesign — Focus Lab](https://www.focuslab.agency/work/aptible).
  *(The rename mapping — Enclave→Deploy and Gridiron→Comply — is stated by Aptible's
  own rebrand post and corroborated by Focus Lab. **Could not verify** the exact
  date of the rebrand announcement; the Series A press in June 2019 already uses
  "Comply" and "Deploy (formerly Enclave)," so the rebrand predates mid-2019.)*

### June 2019 — $12M Series A and the repositioning toward compliance-first

- **June 18, 2019 — $12M Series A, led by Maverick Ventures.** Aptible announced a
  **$12 million Series A** led by **Maverick Ventures**, with participation from
  **Thrive Capital** and **Western Technology Investment (WTI)**, and used the
  moment to launch/relaunch **Aptible Comply** as a "small business security
  management platform." The release reported **150 companies** using **Comply** and
  **250** using **Deploy (formerly Enclave)**, and quoted CEO/co-founder **Chas
  Ballew** ("We've taken everything we've learned about security and compliance and
  turned that into Aptible Comply") and CTO **Frank Macreery** ("We've designed
  Aptible Comply to act as a company's first security hire").
  Sources: [Aptible Closes $12 Million Series A Funding, Launches Small Business Security Management Platform — GlobeNewswire (Jun 18, 2019)](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2019/06/18/1870137/0/en/Aptible-Closes-12-Million-Series-A-Funding-Launches-Small-Business-Security-Management-Platform.html);
  [Announcing Aptible's $12 million Series A funding! — Aptible blog (Jun 18, 2019)](https://www.aptible.com/blog/announcing-aptible-12-million-series-a-funding).
  *(SecurityWeek headlined the round as "Aptible Raises $12 Million to Launch
  Compliance Platform," consistent with the compliance-first framing —
  [SecurityWeek](https://www.securityweek.com/aptible-raises-12-million-launch-compliance-platform/). The
  lead is reported as "Maverick Ventures" in Aptible's own post and "Maverick
  Capital" in the GlobeNewswire wire; these are the same Maverick investing
  group.)*

### 2021 — Conveyor spins out of Aptible Comply

- **2021 — Conveyor spun off; Ballew departs to lead it.** The customer-trust /
  security-questionnaire product that grew out of **Aptible Comply** was spun out
  in **2021** as a separate company, **Conveyor**, with co-founder **Chas Ballew**
  leaving the Aptible CEO seat to run it (he remained on Aptible's board).
  Conveyor describes itself as "born out of the insights obtained building Aptible
  Comply," noting it "started as a free product add-on to the Aptible platform" in
  2021 before becoming its own company.
  Sources: [About Conveyor — Conveyor](https://www.conveyor.com/company/about);
  [Cervin Founder Spotlight: Chas Ballew of Conveyor — Cervin Ventures](https://www.cervinventures.com/news/cervin-founder-spotlight-chas-ballew-of-conveyor-part-1);
  [Conveyor uses AI to automate the painful process of vendor security reviews and RFPs — TechCrunch (Jun 12, 2025)](https://techcrunch.com/2025/06/12/conveyor-uses-ai-to-automate-the-painful-process-of-vendor-security-reviews-and-rfps-with-ai/).

### ~2024–2025 — Aptible exits the GRC business

- **Aptible leaves the GRC/compliance-software space.** Aptible moved on from the
  governance-risk-compliance software market (the **Comply** product), with
  **Drata** positioned as the migration path for existing Comply customers: Drata's
  partner page states it is "partnering with Aptible to provide a seamless
  migration path for Comply customers as Aptible moves on from the GRC space."
  Aptible refocused on its **Deploy** PaaS for regulated/healthcare teams.
  Source: [Migrating from Aptible Comply to Drata for GRC — Drata](https://drata.com/aptible).
  *(The "moves on from the GRC space" wording is Drata's. **Could not verify** an
  exact date for Aptible's GRC exit or whether Comply was sold vs. wound down; the
  Drata migration partnership is the clearest primary marker.)*

### November 2025 — Acquired by Opti9 Technologies

- **November 19–20, 2025 — Opti9 Technologies acquires Aptible.** **Opti9**, an
  **AWS Premier Partner** and managed-cloud provider, announced it had acquired
  Aptible, framing it as combining Opti9's cloud infrastructure with "Aptible's
  compliant and secure PaaS foundation." Co-founder **Frank Macreery** (described
  as CEO/CTO) joined Opti9's executive team. The release recapped Aptible as
  "Founded in 2013 with the original mission to simplify HIPAA compliance for
  healthcare developers," now handling HIPAA, HITRUST, SOC 2, and ISO 27001.
  Opti9's CEO **Paul Butcher** was quoted on the combination; **BDO** advised on
  the deal. Terms were not disclosed.
  Sources: [Opti9 Acquires Aptible to Deliver Next-Generation Compliant Cloud Solutions — Opti9 (Nov 19, 2025)](https://opti9tech.com/news/opti9-acquires-aptible-to-deliver-next-generation-compliant-cloud-solutions/);
  [Opti9's Acquisition of Aptible Signals New Era for Hybrid Cloud and Secure PaaS — Data Center POST (Nov 20, 2025)](https://datacenterpost.com/opti9s-acquisition-of-aptible-signals-new-era-for-hybrid-cloud-and-secure-paas/);
  [Hybrid Cloud Provider Opti9 Acquires Aptible … — Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/hybrid-cloud-provider-opti9-acquires-130300642.html).
  *(Opti9's own release is dated **Nov 19, 2025**; Data Center POST says the deal
  was "announced on **November 20, 2025**." Opti9 is reported as a portfolio
  company of **Crest Rock Partners** — [Crest Rock Partners news](https://www.crestrockpartners.com/news-2);

## Notes on framing and characterization

> Separating **established fact** from **reported framing / brief-supplied claims**,
> with attribution.

- **"Heroku for regulated/healthcare workloads."** This is an accurate
  characterization of Enclave/Deploy: a twelve-factor, Docker-based PaaS with
  compliance controls built in, marketed against Heroku for HIPAA/SOC 2 use cases
  — Aptible's own content compares itself to Heroku and positions itself for
  regulated teams.

  [Heroku and HIPAA — Aptible](https://www.aptible.com/heroku-alternatives/heroku-hipaa).

  and the YC blog both say **Summer 2014 (S14)**.
  Sources: [YC profile](https://www.ycombinator.com/companies/aptible);
  [YC blog](https://www.ycombinator.com/blog/aptible-yc-s14-handles-the-hard-parts-of-hipaa-compliance/).

- **"Series A" framing.** The **$12M** June 2019 round is labeled a **Series A** by
  both Aptible and the wire release. The earlier **$120K** (Aug 2014) is a seed
  round.
  Sources: [GlobeNewswire (Jun 18, 2019)](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2019/06/18/1870137/0/en/Aptible-Closes-12-Million-Series-A-Funding-Launches-Small-Business-Security-Management-Platform.html);
