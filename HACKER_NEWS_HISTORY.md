## Origins (2007-2008)

### February 2007: Launch as "Startup News"

Paul Graham announces the site:

> "We created a new site called Startup News for startup-related news. It's like Reddit, but written in Arc."

**Original purpose:**
- Test Arc (Lisp dialect) in production
- Create a community for Y Combinator founders
- Replace the scattered startup news ecosystem

**Initial design:**
- Written in Arc by Paul Graham and Robert Morris
- Minimalist text-only interface
- Lisp-powered ranking algorithms
- No comments initially

### March 2007: Comments Added

The ability to comment transforms the site from a link aggregator to a discussion forum. Early comments focus on:
- YC application advice
- Technical startup challenges
- Lisp advocacy

### The Name Change

Originally "Startup News," renamed to "Hacker News" to:
- Broaden scope beyond just startups
- Appeal to the "hacker" ethos (in the original sense)
- Avoid being seen as purely YC marketing

## Early Growth (2008-2010)

### 2008: The First Legendary Threads

**Dropbox Show HN (September 2007, major traction 2008):**
- Drew Houston posts early prototype
- Community skeptical but intrigued
- Becomes canonical YC success story

**GitHub Show HN (2008):**
- Pre-launch feedback shapes product
- Community immediately recognizes value

### The "patio11" Arrives (November 2008)

Patrick McKenzie joins HN. His early comments on:
- Japanese business culture
- Micro-ISV operations
- Marketing for developers

Would eventually make him the site's most respected commenter.

### 2009: Stripe Launch

Patrick and John Collison engage with HN community:
- Payment processing for developers
- Documentation critique drives improvements
- Community becomes early adopters

### 2010: The "Falsehoods" Post

patio11's comment about name handling becomes canonical:

> "Falsehoods Programmers Believe About Names"

Originally an HN comment, expanded into a blog post that remains required reading for software engineers.

## The Eternal September Resistance (2010-2013)

### The Problem

Usenet's "Eternal September" (1993) destroyed community quality when AOL connected to the internet. HN faces similar pressures as:
- Reddit grows and declines
- Tech industry expands massively
- Mainstream media discovers tech

### HN's Defenses

**Karma Thresholds Implemented:**
- 31 karma: Flag posts
- 251 karma: Customize header color (easter egg)
- 501 karma: Downvote comments

**Ranking Algorithm Adjustments:**
- Requires 4+ upvotes to reach front page
- Time decay function tuned
- Voting ring detection improved

**Cultural Norms Enforced:**
- "Don't be snarky" guideline
- Downvoting of low-effort comments
- Self-policing by established users

### 2011: Daniel Gackle Joins (as gruseom)

Future moderator dang joins HN:
- Startup background (failed spreadsheet company)
- Stanford literature major
- Gradually takes on moderation duties

### 2012: Major Traffic Growth

SimilarWeb data shows HN surpassing:
- TechCrunch
- Engadget
- Traditional tech media

**Why it worked:**
- Reddit's decline creates refugee population
- High-quality discussion attracts experts
- YC brand draws startup ecosystem

## The dang Era Begins (2013-2016)

### 2013: pg Announces dang as Full-Time Moderator

Paul Graham's announcement:

> "I'm delighted to announce that Daniel Gackle (pronounced Gackley), who has already been doing most of the moderation for the last 18 months, is going to join YC full-time to be in charge of the HN community. Many HN users know Daniel as gruseom, though now he's going to switch to the slightly more legit sounding dang."

**Why this mattered:**
- First dedicated HN employee
- Professionalized moderation
- Personal touch at scale

### 2013: The "Shadowban" Controversy

Users discover HN uses "shadowbanning" (secretly hiding problematic users' posts). Community debates:
- Transparency vs. effectiveness
- dang's explanation: prevents evasion
- Eventually accepted as necessary tool

### 2014-2015: The First "HN is Declining" Threads

Peak "Eternal September" anxiety:
- Threads complaining about quality appear monthly
- Meta-discussions about moderation
- dang's patient explanations of mechanisms

**Pattern established:**
These threads become a ritual themselves, appearing every 18-24 months.

### 2015: Elon Musk AMA

One of the highest-traffic threads in HN history:
- SpaceX technical details
- Tesla discussions
- AI safety concerns
- Establishes HN as venue for serious tech figures

## Maturation & Scale (2016-2019)

### 2016: patio11 Joins Stripe

Patrick McKenzie's hiring noted by community:
- Recognition of his HN contributions
- Stripe's continued HN engagement
- Validates "HN as career accelerator"

### 2017: The Google Memo Incident

James Damore's internal Google memo leaked:
- One of largest threads in HN history
- Heavy moderation required
- Political divisions stress community
- dang's moderation widely discussed

**Outcome:**
- Thread eventually locked
- Ongoing community divisions
- Debate about HN's handling of political topics

### 2018: 10 Million Monthly Visits

SimilarWeb confirms HN milestone:
- 10M+ monthly visits
- Outpaces traditional tech media
- Just two servers (quad-core Xeon E5-2637 v4)

**Technical achievement:**
- Minimal infrastructure
- Arc language proving scalable
- FreeBSD deployment

### 2019: The New Yorker Profile

"The Lonely Work of Moderating Hacker News" by Anna Wiener:

**Key revelations:**
- dang's background as Stanford lit major
- His moderation philosophy
- The personal toll of constant moderation
- "Conversational art" description

**Impact:**
- Mainstream recognition of HN's importance
- Increased appreciation for moderation
- "Thank you dang" threads intensify

### 2019: sctb Departs

Scott Bell (sctb) steps back from moderation:
- Co-founder with dang of failed startup
- Moving to other projects
- End of an era

## The Modern Era (2020-2026)

### 2020: COVID-19 and Remote Work Discussions

HN becomes central forum for:
- Remote work policy debates
- Tech industry layoffs
- Startup survival strategies
- "Tell HN: I got fired" support threads

**Traffic surge:**
- Tech workers stuck at home
- Increased commenting
- New wave of users

### 2021: Cryptocurrency Mania

Bitcoin/crypto threads reach peak volume:
- Early HN skeptics vs. new believers
- "Told you so" vs. "You missed it"
- Environmental concerns debated
- NFT discussions begin

### 2022: ChatGPT Launch

November 2022: OpenAI launches ChatGPT

**HN response:**
- Immediate technical deep dives
- Skepticism about capabilities
- Recognition of paradigm shift
- Ongoing AI safety debates

**The LeCun vs. Yudkowsky debate (2023):**
- Takes place partly on HN-adjacent Twitter
- References HN discussions
- Community follows closely

### 2023: Economic Downturn

"Who is Hiring" thread analysis:
- Lowest post count in 30 months
- Layoff discussions increase
- Startup funding winter
- Community supports affected members

### 2024: AI Tool Integration Discussions

- Claude Code, Cursor, Copilot threads
- "Vibe coding" debates
- Developer workflow changes
- Concerns about skill atrophy

### 2025: Tom Howard Announced as Public Moderator

dang announces:

> "Tom Howard (tomhow) has become a public moderator. He has been performing moderation tasks for years behind the scenes."

**Background:**
- YC W09 batchmate
- Joined HN in 2007
- Long history with community

## Technical Evolution

### The Arc Language

HN runs on Arc, created by Paul Graham and Robert Morris:

**Philosophy:**
- Minimalist Lisp dialect
- Designed for exploratory programming
- Powers HN's unique features

**Source:** http://arclanguage.org/

**Status:**
- Open source but not widely adopted
- HN is the primary Arc application
- Occasional updates from pg

### Infrastructure Scaling

**2007-2010:** Single server
**2010-2015:** Growth necessitates changes
**2015-present:** Two servers

**Current specs (as of 2025):**
- Two machines
- Quad-core Intel Xeon E5-2637 v4
- FreeBSD
- Surprisingly minimal for traffic volume

### Algorithm Changes

**Ranking formula evolution:**
```
(score) / (time_since_submission ^ gravity)
```

**Key adjustments over time:**
- Gravity factor tuned
- Comment ranking separated
- Vote ring detection added
- Second-chance pool mechanism

### API Development

**2014: Official API launched**
- JSON endpoints
- Read-only initially
- Later expanded

**Algolia partnership:**
- Search powered by Algolia (YC alum)
- Real-time indexing
- Advanced query syntax

**BigQuery dataset:**
- Full public dataset
- Enables research
- Community analysis projects

## Cultural Milestones

### The "HN Tone" Evolution

**2007-2010:** Lisp/startup focused, technical
**2010-2015:** Broader tech, culture wars emerge
**2015-2020:** Maturation, professionalization
**2020-2026:** Mainstream awareness, AI focus

**Persistent characteristics:**
- "Performative erudition" (New Yorker, 2019)
- Skepticism of hype
- Respect for technical depth
- Anti-marketing sentiment

### Famous Copypastas & Memes

**"HN is not your target market"**
- Origin: Repeated warnings to startups
- Usage: Reality check for Show HN posts

**"This is obvious if you think about it"**
- Origin: Typical HN response pattern
- Usage: Mocking overconfidence

**"Correlation is not causation"**
- Origin: Statistical discussions
- Usage: Generic dismissal (sometimes unfairly)

**"You should grow your email list" (patio11)**
- Origin: patio11's frequent advice
- Usage: Default business recommendation

### The "Deadpool"

HN tracks failed startups with particular interest:
- Post-mortem analyses valued
- "What went wrong" discussions
- More educational than success stories (per community)

**Notable deadpool discussions:**
- Homejoy (2015)
- Theranos (unfolding scandal)
- Various crypto projects (2018, 2022)

### Post Statistics

**Median score of posts:**
- Most posts receive 0-1 upvotes
- Only ~10% reach front page
- Top post daily: typically 200-500 points
- Exceptional posts: 1000+ points

**Comment statistics:**
- Top threads: 1000+ comments
- Average thread: 20-50 comments
- Median comment score: 1-2 points

### User Growth

**Registered users:**
- Estimated 5M+ accounts
- Active commenters: ~50,000/month
- Heavy contributors: ~5,000

**Karma distribution:**
- 0-100: Majority of users
- 100-1000: Regular contributors
- 1000-10000: Established voices
- 10000+: Legendary status (patio11: 127k+)

### Content Analysis

**Topic distribution (per Wilson Lin analysis):**
- Programming: 25%
- Startups/Business: 20%
- Science: 15%
- AI/ML: 15% (increasing)
- Other tech: 15%
- Miscellaneous: 10%

**Temporal patterns:**
- Peak activity: Tuesday-Thursday, 8-10am PT
- Lowest activity: Weekends
- "Who is Hiring" threads: Massive traffic spikes
