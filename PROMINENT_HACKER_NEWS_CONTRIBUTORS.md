## Quality Indicators

### Green Flags (Credible Contributors)

1. **Cites specific versions/dates:** "In Claude 3.5 Sonnet (December 2024)..."
2. **Discloses biases:** "I work in this space, but..."
3. **Provides reproducible examples:** Code snippets, specific prompts
4. **Acknowledges limitations:** "This works for X but not Y"
5. **References primary sources:** Links to papers, official docs
6. **Consistent expertise:** Same handle, same domain knowledge over time
7. **Admits mistakes:** "I was wrong about..."

### Red Flags (Questionable Contributors)

1. **Anonymous accounts with new accounts:** Recently created, strong opinions
2. **Binary thinking:** "X is completely useless" or "X replaces all developers"
3. **No specific examples:** Only general claims
4. **Venture hype patterns:** Uncritical promotion of specific companies
5. **Credential dropping without substance:** "As a senior engineer at FAANG..."
6. **Emotional language:** "Anyone who doesn't see this is delusional"
7. **Unsolicited product promotion:** "That's why I built [product]"

## Research Methodology

This analysis is based on:

1. **Thread analysis:** 6 major AI coding tool threads (Dec 2025 - Mar 2026)
2. **Comment tracking:** 1000+ comments analyzed
3. **Cross-reference:** Verified identities where publicly confirmed
4. **Pattern recognition:** Recurring themes and contributor behaviors
5. **Time-series:** Evolution of opinions over 2024-2026

**Limitations:**
- Pseudonymous culture prevents complete attribution
- Self-selection bias (HN users skew technical, Silicon Valley-adjacent)
- Temporal bias (AI discussions have increased dramatically since 2022)
- Survivorship bias (accounts that have persisted over time)

---

## Key Findings

1. **Quality polarization:** High-quality technical discussions coexist with hype-driven comments
2. **Tool tribalism:** Users tend to defend their chosen tools (Claude Code vs. Codex vs. Aider)
3. **Creator engagement:** Tool creators who engage on HN (Raad, Hashimoto) receive more goodwill
4. **Skepticism dominance:** HN culture favors skeptical takes; optimistic claims face scrutiny
5. **Experience correlation:** Contributors with production experience tend to be more cautious
