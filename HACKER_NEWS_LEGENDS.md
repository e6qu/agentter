## The Legendary Threads

### The Dropbox Show HN (2007)

**Submission:** "Show HN: Dropbox - Throw away your USB drive"

**What happened:**
- Single-founder startup (Drew Houston) posted prototype
- Top comment: "For a Linux user, you can already build something like Dropbox with inotify/rsync... I don't see why this has any value."
- Another comment: "You can already build something like this with FTP"
- Valuation today: ~$8B (as of 2025)

**Why it matters:**
- Demonstrates the "HN is not your market" phenomenon
- Shows experts can be wrong about consumer products
- Often cited when dismissing new ideas

**Link:** https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=9224

### "Falsehoods Programmers Believe About Names" (2010)

**Author:** Patrick McKenzie (patio11)

**Key points:**
- People have exactly one canonical name
- Names don't change
- Names are unique
- Names fit in ASCII
- (42 total falsehoods)

**Impact:**
- Now a genre ("Falsehoods Programmers Believe About...")
- Referenced in database design discussions
- Demonstrates importance of domain expertise

**Link:** https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=1759344

### The "Who Is Hiring?" Series

**Started:** January 2011
**Format:** Monthly on first weekday
**Posted by:** whoishiring (official bot)

**Evolution:**
- Originally organic community posts
- Standardized format (location, remote, visa)
- Became economic indicator (tech job trends)
- Peak: 1000+ top-level comments per month

**Cultural significance:**
- Most reliable tech hiring data source
- Shows industry trends (AI jobs in 2023+)
- Companies compete for attention in threads

### The Aaron Swartz Memorial Thread (2013)

**Date:** January 11, 2013
**Submission:** "Aaron Swartz has died"
**Points:** 4000+ (among highest ever)
**Comments:** 800+

**Context:**
- Co-founder of Reddit, RSS, Creative Commons
- Facing federal charges for JSTOR downloads
- Committed suicide at age 26

**Discussion themes:**
- Prosecutorial overreach
- Mental health in tech
- Open access to knowledge
- Legacy of internet activism

**Tone:** Rare moment of consensus and grief

### The Google Diversity Memo (2017)

**Date:** August 2017
**Submission:** "Google's Ideological Echo Chamber"
**Status:** Highly controversial, heavily moderated

**What happened:**
- Internal Google memo leaked
- Biological differences in tech interest claimed
- James Damore fired
- Discussion immediately heated

**HN response:**
- Thread locked/penalized quickly
- Multiple follow-up threads
- Debate about "politics on HN"
- dang actively moderated throughout

**Outcome:**
- Damore lawsuit settled 2020
- Became touchstone for "cancel culture" debates
- HN policy on politics tightened

### Elon Musk AMA (2015)

**Date:** January 2015
**Title:** "I am Elon Musk, ask me anything"
**Points:** 7000+
**Comments:** 2000+

**Highlights:**
- Mars colonization timeline
- OpenAI founding (before the split)
- Tesla battery tech
- "AI is potentially more dangerous than nukes"

**Atmosphere:** Generally positive, pre-Twitter acquisition

### The "Please Stop" Thread (2012)

**Submission:** Various "Please stop posting X" metas
**Pattern:** Recurring complaint format

**Common complaints:**
- "Please stop posting Bitcoin stories"
- "Please stop posting NSA stories"
- "Please stop posting political stories"

**dang's response (typical):**
> "The solution to a story you don't like is to not read it. The solution to too many stories of a type is to submit better ones."

### The Stripe CTF Series

**Years:** 2012, 2014
**Format:** Capture The Flag security competitions

**Significance:**
- Educational security challenges
- Community participation
- Stripe's developer relations strategy
- Now legendary status among security folks

### The "I Sold My Startup" Posts

**Genre:** Acquisition announcements

**Notable examples:**
- "We sold our startup for $X" (various)
- Behind-the-scenes stories
- Financial transparency (sometimes)

**Common reactions:**
- Congratulations
- "What's your tech stack?"
- "How did you acquire users?"
- "What's next?"

### The "HN Hug of Death"

**Phenomenon:**
- Small site hits front page
- Traffic overwhelms server
- Site goes down
- Community discusses alternative hosting

**Examples:**
- Personal blogs on shared hosting
- New services with no CDN
- Weekend projects
- Academic papers on university servers

**Community response:**
- Archive.org links
- Cached versions
- "Did we hug it to death?"
- Technical post-mortems

### Common Show HN Patterns

**The "How I built X" format:**
- Technical deep dive
- Stack explanation
- Challenges overcome
- Revenue/usage numbers (sometimes)

**The "I quit my job to build X" format:**
- Dramatic narrative
- Mixed reception (admiration vs. skepticism)
- Often asked about financial runway

**The "X but simpler/faster/cheaper" format:**
- Dropbox alternative
- GitHub alternative
- Google Docs alternative
- High bar for differentiation

## The Deadpool

### Failed Show HNs (Famous Examples)

**Pattern:** Highly upvoted, then vanished

**Common causes:**
1. **Voting ring detection** - Coordinated upvotes
2. **Domain ban** - Site on spam list
3. **Self-promotion rules** - Excessive promotion
4. **Duplicate** - Previously posted
5. **Controversy penalty** - Too many comments too fast

### Notable Banned Domains

**Historical bans (some lifted):**
- Business Insider (various periods)
- Medium (spam period)
- Some Substack newsletters
- Certain cryptocurrency sites

**How bans happen:**
- Repeated spam submissions
- Vote manipulation
- Pay-for-play content
- dang: "We ban sites that try to game the system"

### The "Second Chance Pool"

**What it is:**
- Rescued from dead/flagged status
- Curated by moderators
- Second chance at front page

**Notable rescues:**
- Quality posts initially flagged unfairly
- Technical content misunderstood
- Controversial but important topics

## Memes and Catchphrases

### "HN is not your market"

**Origin:** Response to criticism of consumer products

**Context:**
- HN users are developers, not typical consumers
- Technical criticism ≠ market validation
- Dropbox, others initially dismissed

**Usage:**
> "HN commenters are saying it's too simple, but HN is not your market."

### "This."

**Meaning:** "I strongly agree with this statement"

**Evolution:**
- Initially: Simple agreement
- Later: Mocked as low-effort
- Now: Parodied or used ironically

### "Rent seeking"

**HN usage:** Critique of business models

**Original meaning:** Economic term (unearned income)
**HN meaning:** Any business charging for value
**Status:** Often misused, mocked when used incorrectly

### "Skin in the game"

**From:** Taleb (Antifragile)
**Usage:** Arguing about incentives
**Common thread:** Debates about founder/VC alignment

### "patio11's Law"

**Formulation:** "The information is not hiding, it is just priced in a different currency than money"

**Context:** Knowledge arbitrage opportunities
**Example:** Japan fax machines, regulatory arbitrage

### "Teach yourself X in Y days"

**Origin:** "Teach Yourself C++ in 21 Days"
**HN genre:** "Teach Yourself CS" - actually good
**Status:** Parodied constantly

### "Move fast and break things"

**From:** Facebook
**HN reaction:** Mostly critical
**Alternative:** "Move slowly and fix things" (patio11)

### "That's not agile"

**Usage:** Mocking cargo cult agile
**Context:** Startup methodology debates
**Paired with:** "But we have standups!"

### "Vibe coding"

**Origin:** 2024-2025 AI coding trend
**Meaning:** Programming by describing intent to AI
**HN reaction:** Mixed (productivity tool vs. skill degradation)

## Notable Departures and Drama

### The SQLite Code of Conduct (2018)

**Event:** SQLite temporarily adopted a "Code of Conduct" based on religious rules

**HN reaction:**
- Immediate backlash
- Debate about maintainer autonomy
- Later reverted

### The npm Left-Pad Incident (2016)

**Event:**
- Developer unpublished 11-line package
- Broke thousands of projects
- npm restored package

**HN discussion themes:**
- Dependency fragility
- Open source sustainability
- Corporate responsibility
- JavaScript ecosystem criticism

### Various User Departures

**Pattern:** High-karma user announces leaving

**Common reasons cited:**
- Toxicity increase
- Too much AI/politics content
- Moderation disagreements
- "Not what it used to be"

**dang's typical response:**
> "Sorry to see you go. Feel free to return anytime."

### The "HN is Dying" Threads

**Frequency:** Approximately quarterly
**Predicted causes:**
- Eternal September
- AI content flood
- Political polarization
- Startup culture decline

**Reality:**
- HN traffic continues growing
- Quality maintained (per dang's stats)
- Same complaints since 2008
